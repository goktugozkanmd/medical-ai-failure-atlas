# Hospital AI Literacy Collaboration Packet

Date: 2026 06 19

Status: public one page route owner packet for hospital, medical faculty, simulation, health informatics, ethics, quality, and data governance review.

Purpose: help a hospital or medical faculty route owner decide whether a short clinician AI safety literacy conversation is useful before any formal proposal, patient data decision, clinical use, or institutional commitment.

This packet is not a hospital partnership, not an official course, not accredited training, not a clinical protocol, not clinical validation, not clinical deployment, not patient data use, not ethics approval, not procurement evidence, not model ranking, not score certification, not an application, not a proposal, not payment, and not terms acceptance.

## Thirty minute review route

1. Five minute scope check: confirm that only public examples, synthetic rows, and public claim language are in scope.
2. Ten minute literacy check: review source support, uncertainty, abstention, no ranking language, and blocked public claims.
3. Ten minute governance check: review data boundary, owner role, risk register, escalation path, and stop conditions.
4. Five minute route decision: name a better owner, request a narrower packet, or mark no fit.

## Review objects

1. Readiness matrix: route owner, eligibility, non patient data, Turkish medical LLM evaluation, clinician literacy, health data quality, governance, sandbox boundary, and opportunity package.
2. Mini curriculum: short clinician review object for source support, uncertainty, unsafe public wording, and no ranking language.
3. Governance intake worksheet: public intake row for policy, owner, data, third party, education, feedback, and transparency.
4. No ranking warning: public guardrail that blocks score, leaderboard, demo, or fluent answer from becoming safety proof.

## Minimum useful outcome

A useful first outcome is one of these:

1. Correct owner named.
2. Not fit confirmed.
3. Missing evidence named.
4. Better public packet requested.
5. One bounded review meeting requested.

## Stop conditions

Stop before any of these:

1. Patient data.
2. Private hospital operational data.
3. Clinical workflow use.
4. Clinical validation wording.
5. Institutional endorsement wording.
6. Procurement or vendor evaluation wording.
7. Ethics approval implication.
8. Course approval or accreditation wording.
9. Funding or budget wording.
10. Formal application wording.
11. Partner commitment wording.
12. Official role wording.
13. Model ranking or score certification.

## Current opportunity interpretation

1. TÜSEB A4 UM is time sensitive, but route owner and eligibility remain unknown.
2. TÜBİTAK 1711 is larger and longer, but it needs a real consortium route and a smart education fit if used.
3. A hospital or medical faculty literacy packet is the smallest useful bridge between public open source infrastructure and a serious route owner conversation.
4. This packet should be shared only as a route fit object, not as a proposal or institutional claim.

## Decision needed from route owner

The only requested decision is: which route should review a non patient data clinician AI safety literacy object, and what evidence would make the next conversation useful.
