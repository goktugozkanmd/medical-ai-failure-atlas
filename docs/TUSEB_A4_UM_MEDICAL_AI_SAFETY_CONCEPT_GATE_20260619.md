## Concept fit

### Fit lane 1: Turkish medical LLM safety evaluation

Possible concept: build a non patient data pilot for Turkish medical LLM failure mode capture, source support review, no ranking reporting, and clinician review protocol readiness.

Why it may fit: it is led by medical safety questions and can remain short term if scoped to public or synthetic material.

Blockers before proposal: exact call text, institutional executor route, human reviewer plan, and decision on whether any patient data is completely excluded.

### Fit lane 2: clinician AI safety literacy

Possible concept: build a compact clinician AI literacy module focused on source support, hallucination risk, public claim hygiene, and no ranking safety reporting.

Why it may fit: education and adoption readiness can support safer health AI use without claiming clinical deployment.

Blockers before proposal: host education route, target clinician group, human teaching ownership, and whether TÜYZE or another unit is the correct route.

### Fit lane 3: health data quality and label audit

Possible concept: build a health data stewardship and label audit worksheet for AI readiness decisions before model evaluation.

Why it may fit: data quality and label uncertainty are central to medical AI safety, and can be scoped without patient data at first stage.

Blockers before proposal: route owner, permission boundary, data type boundary, and whether the first stage uses only synthetic or public non patient examples.
